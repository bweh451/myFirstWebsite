The ultimate burger

Why a burger?

It's simple. Burgers have been one of my go to food items to get on a menu, but I felt like they have become overpriced for what you get. This recipe will showcase how to make an incredibly delecious burger, that is simple and straight to the point. For a decent price !

Ingredients Quantity
500g Wagyu beef mince 2
4-pack Seeded white hamburger rolls 1
150g Lettuce 1
Large Tomato 1
12-pack Processed cheddar cheese slices 1
150g Bwell Mayonaise 1
Coconut Oil 1 tbsp
Salt & Pepper To taste

Step 1:

Prepare all ingredients. Wash and dry lettuce. Remove cheese slices from packaging. Wash and slice tomato. Slide hamburger buns in half.

Step 2:

Prepare the patties. Cut parchment paper into squares (about 10cm x 10cm). Lightly shape minced meat into 125g balls. Layer the balls between two sheets of parchment paper and flatten to about 1.5cm. Remove top parchment paper and season patties with salt and pepper to taste.

Step 3:

Shallow fry the patties. Add 1 tbsp. of coconut oil to a frying pan. Bring the oil to a medium-high heat. Add your room temperature flattened patties to the fryin pan, seasoned side down. Remove parchment paper and season with salt and pepper to taste. Fry until the blood start to pool on the top of the patties, flip and add two slices of cheese. Remove from pan when underside is crisp.

Step 4:

Toast buns. Using the beef fat that's left over in the pan. add your top and bottom buns with their inside side facing down. Toast until golden brown. Remove from heat and spread 1 tbsp. of mayonnaise on the bottom bun.

Step 5:

Assemble the burger as follows:
  1. Bottom bun with mayonnaise.
  2. Two pieces of lettuce.
  3. Add two patties with their melted cheese.
  4. Add on slice of tomato.
  5. Add your favourite sauce.
  6. Add top bun.
